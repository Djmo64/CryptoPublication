Why it should be taxed: first and foremost, all crypto currency is classified as property in the united states, it is compared to stuff like having gold or anything of value to trade for goods and services. If it generates a capital gain your bound to be taxed for it. The 16th amendment states “The Congress shall have power to lay and collect taxes on incomes, from whatever source derived, without apportionment among the several states, and without regard to any census or enumeration.” this applies to crypto as well this is intangible property that fits under the parameters of U.S. law. However, cryptocurrency is only taxed when a transaction is made with it, in other words a ‘taxable event’ so just having it in your possession does not make mean you have to pay a tax for it every year despite it being classified as property. 80% of government revenue comes from income tax, this is money that provides things such as public schooling, police forces, museums/public parks, and national defense. As well as paying government officials in order to make and pass laws across the country. Cryptocurrency is reaching the point where digital currency that is not under the jurisdiction of a government is being used by more and more people and the possibility of the government revenue.
